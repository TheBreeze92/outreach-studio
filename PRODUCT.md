# Product

## Register

product

## Users

B2B sales professionals, founders, and growth operators who send cold outreach at volume. They use this mid-workflow — prospect identified, LinkedIn profile pulled, now they need a first-contact email that doesn't read like a template. Context: focused, fast-moving, results-oriented. They judge the tool by the quality of the email it produces, not the UI that surrounds it.

## Product Purpose

Cold Outreach Studio turns a LinkedIn PDF into a high-signal, personalized cold email using a 6-part proprietary framework. The app researches the prospect and finds a real, recent signal (news, funding, hire, launch) then writes the email around that signal. The user verifies and sends. Success: an email that the prospect reads, believes is written for them specifically, and responds to.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Confident · Precise. The tool of a seasoned closer. No noise, no fluff — just signal. The interface should feel like it was built by someone who sends cold email for a living, not by a startup that sells to sales teams. Authoritative without being aggressive. Credible without being corporate.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS cream/purple**: HubSpot, Apollo, Instantly — soft gradients, rounded cards, pastel purple CTAs. This reads as "another sales tool." Avoid.
- **Corporate enterprise**: Salesforce, LinkedIn itself — navy + white, stiff grid, small text, soul-free. This reads as something the IT team chose, not something a rep would choose for themselves.

## Design Principles

1. **The output is the product.** Every UI decision should make the generated email easier to read, verify, and act on. Chrome that distracts from the output is chrome that should not exist.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** Sharp typography and precise spacing communicate authority. Decorative elements, gradients, and rounded softness undermine it.
3. **Earned weight.** Color, shadow, and size are used to create hierarchy — not decoration. The most important thing on screen should look most important.
4. **Never apologize for the tool.** No softening language, no "AI may make mistakes" disclaimers littering the UI. This is a professional instrument; present it as one.
5. **Signal over noise.** Every screen has one primary action. Secondary information recedes. The user should never have to decide what to look at.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text must achieve ≥4.5:1 contrast. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Reduced motion respected — animations must have `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives.
